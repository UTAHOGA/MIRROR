U.O.G.A. Standard Operating Procedures

(S.O.P.s) for Vetted and Certified Professional Outfitters (C.P.O.s)

Mission-Aligned, Not Empty Marketing

U.O.G.A. intends these designations to mean something. A published designation should communicate that an outfitter is part of a stronger, more trustworthy resource built around professionalism, clarity, and credible public-facing standards.

Designation Definitions

Two public levels, one internal hold state.

Vetted

U.O.G.A.'s baseline public review designation. It indicates that U.O.G.A. reviewed the outfitter's submitted business and operating information and compared it, where possible, with available public, government, and other relevant source data.

Certified Professional Outfitter (C.P.O.)

U.O.G.A.'s Professional Operator designation for outfitters who meet or exceed U.O.G.A.'s higher qualification criteria, informed by national licensing and professional competency benchmarks.

Records that remain incomplete, unresolved, or unreviewed are treated internally as unpublished and unverified. They are not publicly displayed with a designation.

Why It Exists

Value-added, mission-driven, and built to raise the bar.

U.O.G.A. wants public designation to create meaningful value in the market. For operators, that means a stronger public trust signal, more credible differentiation, and better alignment with the mission and values of the organization. For clients, it means greater confidence that a listed outfitter reflects a more serious level of professionalism, readiness, and business credibility than a name gathered from a raw list.

  • Stronger public confidence in the U.O.G.A. resource
  • More meaningful professional distinction for outfitters
  • Clearer market signal for quality, readiness, and standing
  • A higher and more aspirational benchmark through C.P.O.

Vetted Criteria

Baseline standard for trusted public publication

  • Business identity information is sufficiently complete
  • Contact information is current enough to support listing
  • Utah registration or licensure information is identified where applicable
  • Services, operating relevance, and authority claims are clearly described
  • Submitted information is reasonably consistent with available source data where such data exists
  • No unresolved major identity or operating-information conflict remains
  • The outfitter is suitable for publication as a trusted resource

C.P.O. Criteria

Higher professional designation for stronger operator standing

  • All Vetted requirements are already satisfied
  • Business and operating profile is more complete and reliable
  • Professionalism, preparedness, responsiveness, and readiness are stronger
  • Licensing, registration, and standing are stronger where applicable
  • Current insurance and business-operating information are stronger where applicable
  • No unresolved material concern exists that would undermine a higher designation
  • The outfitter meets or exceeds U.O.G.A.'s internal professional standard

How Review Works

Structured review grounded in available sources and professional judgment.

1
Outfitter submission

Business identity, contact information, services, operating areas, and authority claims are collected.

2
Source comparison

Where possible, U.O.G.A. compares submitted information with available public, government, and other relevant source data.

3
Review for consistency

Identity, clarity, responsiveness, operational coherence, and overall record quality are evaluated.

4
Publication decision

The outfitter is either held back from publication, designated as Vetted, or designated as C.P.O.

What U.O.G.A. May Review

Sources that help support professional validation

  • Submitted outfitter information
  • Public business presence and contact data
  • Website and social-business identity
  • Government-source information where available
  • Federal or state permit-reference information where available
  • Professional completeness and responsiveness

Utah DWR registration matters. Where public-land compensated operation requires Utah DWR registration, that status forms part of the baseline regulatory floor considered in U.O.G.A.'s review. U.O.G.A. does not replace DWR authority, but public designation should reflect whether that floor has been clearly addressed.

Important Limitation

What a designation does not replace

U.O.G.A. designations are informational and reflect U.O.G.A.'s review process only. They do not constitute a legal determination, permit grant, land-access guarantee, or agency authorization. Final authority remains with the applicable agency, landowner, CWMU, permitting entity, or governing authority.

U.O.G.A. may require updated information, periodic re-review, or removal of a designation if information becomes materially incomplete, inconsistent, outdated, or unreliable. The purpose of this system is to maintain a public resource that reflects the mission, values, and professional expectations of U.O.G.A. while keeping its role and limitations clear.
